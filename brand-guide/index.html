01 — Name & Etymology

The name Phylasso

The name is not invented — it is ancient. Understanding its origin is essential to understanding the brand.

φυλάσσω
phylássō — Ancient Greek · Verb
"to guard, to watch, or to keep safe"

Specifically describes the act of protecting something of great value — keeping it safe, unimpaired, and intact. Not merely watching over it, but actively ensuring nothing is lost.

Colour usage rules

Rules
  • Cyan is the only primary action colour. Do not use other colours for primary buttons or interactive highlights.
  • Gold is reserved exclusively for Pro tier messaging. Do not use it decoratively.
  • Red is reserved for genuine alerts and errors. Do not use it for branding or decoration.
  • Never place cyan text on a light background — contrast is insufficient. Use the deep cyan #0090B8 variant for light-background contexts instead.

05 — Messaging

Brand positioning

Phylasso sits at the intersection of practical household preparedness and intelligent technology. It is not a survival brand, a military brand, or a fear-based brand. It is calm, capable, and built for ordinary families.

Positioning statement

"Phylasso is the offline-first emergency preparedness app for UK and US households — giving families the plans, supplies awareness, and real-time intelligence they need to stay safe when it matters most."

Target audience

🏠
The Prepared Household
Families and couples who want basic preparedness without being overwhelmed. They want clear checklists, simple tracking, and peace of mind.
🎒
The Serious Prepper
Individuals who actively plan for emergencies — bug-out bags, multiple scenarios, supply rotation. They want depth, AI-generated plans, and live threat data.
👨‍👩‍👧
Parents with Young Children
Parents who feel responsible for their family's safety. They want household-specific guidance that accounts for children, dietary needs, and medications.
🌍
The Climate-Aware
People increasingly aware of flood, wildfire, and storm risk. They want real-time local alerts and to understand their specific environmental exposure.

Key messaging pillars

Pillar 1 — Offline First
When the network goes down, Phylasso stays up.
Lead with this in all product and app store copy. It is the most differentiating technical fact and the most emotionally resonant for the target audience — emergencies often disrupt connectivity.
Pillar 2 — Household-focused
Built for your household — not for soldiers, not for agencies.
Always refer to "your household", "your family", "your supplies". Avoid military or institutional language. This is for ordinary people in ordinary homes.
Pillar 3 — AI-powered but trustworthy
Intelligent guidance, not blind automation. Always verify with official sources.
Lean into the AI capability as a feature, but always pair it with the legal disclaimer and the instruction to cross-check with authorities. This builds trust and manages liability.
Pillar 4 — Calm authority
Not alarmist. Not dismissive. Just prepared.
Phylasso exists to reduce anxiety through readiness, not to amplify fear. All copy should feel steady, capable, and matter-of-fact.

07 — Tone of Voice

Tone of voice

How Phylasso communicates is as important as what it communicates. The brand should feel like a knowledgeable, calm friend — not a corporation, not a doomsday preacher, not a tech startup trying too hard.

🧭
Calm & authoritative
Write with confidence, not urgency. Preparedness is a steady habit, not a panic response. Use declarative sentences. Avoid exclamation marks in informational copy.
🏡
Household-first
Always address the reader as a household member, not a soldier, expert, or consumer. "Your household", "your family", "your supplies" — never abstract or corporate.
🌿
Practical, not preachy
Tell people what to do, not why they should feel bad for not doing it. Avoid guilt-driven copy. Focus on enablement — here is what you can do, here is how Phylasso helps.
📖
Plain English
No jargon, no militarisms, no tech buzzwords. If a word could be simpler, make it simpler. Write for a 14-year-old who has never heard of emergency preparedness.

08 — Do & Don't

Do & Don't

Quick reference for the most common brand mistakes to avoid.

✓ Do
  • Write "Phylasso" — capital P, lowercase the rest
  • Use cyan as the sole primary action colour
  • Lead with the offline-first capability
  • Address readers as households or families
  • Use calm, steady, declarative language
  • Always pair AI-generated content with a disclaimer
  • Use the shield mark with adequate clear space
  • Refer to preparedness, not survivalism
  • Keep logo on dark or near-dark backgrounds where possible
  • Use Inter at the approved weights and tracking
✕ Don't
  • Write "PHYLASSO" outside of the logo/wordmark context
  • Use gold for anything other than Pro tier
  • Use fear, urgency, or alarmist language to sell
  • Use military or agency framing ("command", "intelligence")
  • Stretch, rotate, recolour, or add effects to the shield mark
  • Use cyan text on a light or white background
  • Describe users as "survivalists" or use "doomsday" language
  • Present AI-generated content without the legal disclaimer
  • Use the full wordmark tracking (+3px) on body copy
  • Add decorative or third-party logos near the Phylasso mark

11 — Imagery

Imagery guidelines

Until official photography and illustration assets are produced, these principles govern all visual choices in marketing materials, social media, and press.

✅
Household environmentsKitchens, utility rooms, family spaces — real and lived-in. Shows the product in context without dramatisation.
✅
Dark, cinematic toneLow-key lighting, cool colour palette matching brand colours. Avoid bright, lifestyle-stock imagery — it clashes with the brand.
✅
App screenshotsAlways use dark theme. Show real screens, not mockup placeholders. Use device frames that match the brand palette.
❌
Avoid: disaster imageryDo not show floods, fires, or destruction. Phylasso prepares people — it does not exploit fear. No post-apocalyptic or crisis photography.
❌
Avoid: military aestheticNo camouflage, tactical gear, weapons, or uniformed figures. Phylasso is for families, not armed forces.
❌
Avoid: bright stock photosSmiling people on white backgrounds, generic business photography, or overly polished lifestyle imagery that feels inconsistent with the dark theme.
